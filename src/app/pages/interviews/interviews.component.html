Women in Horror Interview

Q: When did you first become a horror fan?
A: I have always been drawn to horror. My maternal grandmother read Edgar Allan Poe tales to me as bedtime stories, so I was introduced to the spooky and macabre at a very young age. Today, I turn to horror as a way of “letting off steam.” After a particularly stressful week, there is nothing I love more than climbing into bed, pulling the covers up, and putting on a really scary movie. It relaxes me...

Q: Tell us about zombies?
A: Zombies interest me because of their cannibalistic compulsion. I feel that women tend to be obsessed with food: with starving themselves, with diets, with equating food with love and acceptance, so I am interested in what happens when the lust for food (in this case, human flesh) overrides the social norms to watch what we eat...
